About Me

Applied economist working at the intersection of agribusiness, food markets, and policy.

Dr. Ly Nguyen, Assistant Professor of Agribusiness at Florida A&M University.

I am an Assistant Professor of Agribusiness in the College of Agriculture and Food Sciences at Florida A&M University. My work uses applied econometrics, Equilibrium Displacement Models (EDM), survey research, choice experiments, and market analysis to examine how consumers, producers, and supply chains respond to changing food systems, agricultural policies, and domestic and international market conditions.

My research interests include price analysis, consumer willingness to pay, specialty crop markets, food labeling, wine economics, international trade policy, and agricultural risk management. I am especially interested in producing research that is useful for producers, agribusiness stakeholders, policymakers, and outreach audiences.

Research

Research areas

My research connects consumer demand, producer decisions, market signals, and agricultural policy through applied economic tools.

01

Consumer Behavior and Willingness to Pay

I examine how consumers value food attributes such as local origin, organic certification, health information, risk reduction, labeling, and product familiarity using survey research and discrete choice experiments.

02

Specialty Crops and Emerging Markets

My work supports the commercialization of emerging and specialty crops by studying consumer demand, market opportunities, producer adoption, and economic feasibility.

03

International Trade and Wine Economics

I analyze trade policy, retaliatory tariffs, trade diversion, and market responses in high value agricultural products, especially wine and specialty food products.

04

Agricultural Policy and Food Systems

I study how policies and market interventions influence food access, shopping behavior, producer welfare, and the broader performance of agricultural markets.

Selected current projects

  • Emerging crops in Florida: Consumer demand, local branding, information treatments, and willingness to pay for emerging fruit and vegetable crops.
  • Florida strawberry market decision tools: Development of market indicators and harvest-shipping guidance to support specialty crop producers.
  • SNAP policy and healthy shopping behavior: Analysis of waivers, nudges, and shopping cart outcomes among SNAP participants.
  • Wine trade policy: Empirical analysis of retaliatory tariffs, trade re-routing, and welfare implications in wine markets.

Teaching

Courses and student engagement

My teaching focuses on helping students connect economic theory, data analysis, and real world agribusiness decisions. I emphasize applied problem solving, policy relevance, and practical research skills.

I enjoy mentoring undergraduate and graduate students through class projects, research experiences, competitions, and professional development opportunities in agribusiness and food systems.
